Story 1

EU ‚ÄúBlocking Statute‚Äù to Shield Iran Trade

7 Aug 2018: The EU activates its updated Blocking Statute (Reg. 2271/96) to counter the extraterritorial reach of U.S. Iran sanctions, creating conflicts-of-law for EU firms.

Read the short extract

The statute prohibits EU persons from complying with specified U.S. secondary sanctions unless authorised, enables recovery of damages, and renders foreign judgments based on the covered measures unenforceable within the EU. Compliance teams must update contractual language, attestations, and escalation paths for clashes between EU obligations and U.S. exposure.

Source: European Union ‚Äî Official Journal L199; European Commission guidance (7 Aug 2018).

Story 3

FinCEN Extends Beneficial Ownership Relief

8 Aug 2018: FinCEN prolongs its temporary exceptive relief for certain CD rollovers and loan renewals/modifications under the Beneficial Ownership Rule.

Read the short extract

The relief responded to operational challenges around re-collecting beneficial ownership information at each rollover. Institutions were expected to adjust policies, procedures, and control testing to reflect the extension period and align with supervision manuals.

Source: FinCEN ‚Äî ‚ÄúExtension of Temporary Exceptive Relief‚Äù (8 Aug 2018).

8 Aug 2018: Malaysian prosecutors file three additional AMLA charges against former PM Najib Razak amid ongoing 1MDB-related cases.

Story 6

Venezuela PDVSA: US$1.2bn Laundering Scheme

Late Jul 2018: U.S. authorities charge conspirators with embezzling and laundering funds via layered transactions, Miami real estate, and sham investments.

The case underscores gatekeeper risks in high-value real estate, the role of shell companies, and cross-border layering. Institutions should revalidate controls over private banking, real estate-linked payments, and high-risk professional intermediaries.
