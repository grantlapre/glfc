27‚Äì29 Jun 2018: Pakistan added to increased monitoring; Iraq and Vanuatu removed; Iran warned; FATF launches work on virtual assets/VASPs.

Read the short extract

At its Paris meeting on 27‚Äì29 June 2018, the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) announced significant changes to its monitoring lists and strategic priorities. Pakistan was formally placed on the ‚Äúincreased monitoring‚Äù list (grey list), reflecting concerns about deficiencies in countering terrorist financing. Story 2

Commonwealth Bank of Australia ‚Äî AU$700m Settlement

4‚Äì20 Jun 2018: AUSTRAC and CBA agree to a record civil penalty for systemic AML/CTF failures; Federal Court approval on 20 June.

Read the short extract

On 4 June 2018, the Commonwealth Bank of Australia (CBA) reached a record AU$700 million settlement with AUSTRAC, later confirmed by the Federal Court on 20 June 2018. The case centered on systemic failures in monitoring and reporting obligations, especially related to the use of Intelligent Deposit Machines (IDMs). CBA admitted to late filing of over 53,000 threshold transaction reports, inadequate monitoring of 778,370 accounts, and 149 missing or late suspicious matter reports. Many lapses involved customers depositing large sums in cash that were linked to organized crime and drug importation. The case set a precedent as the largest corporate civil penalty in Australian history, far surpassing Tabcorp‚Äôs AU$45 million fine in 2017. For AML compliance officers, it highlights regulator expectations for technology‚Äëdriven monitoring, escalation of anomalies, and board‚Äëlevel accountability in remediation programmes.

Source: AUSTRAC v CBA ‚Äî Federal Court filings & orders (Jun 2018).

EBA Probes Malta‚Äôs FIAU Over Pilatus Case

7 Jun 2018: EBA initiates a formal investigation into national AML supervision in Malta following the Pilatus Bank affair.

Read the short extract

On 7 June 2018, the European Banking Authority (EBA) launched a formal investigation into Malta‚Äôs Financial Intelligence Analysis Unit (FIAU) regarding its handling of enforcement against Pilatus Bank. The probe followed widespread concerns that Pilatus, a private bank, had been used to launder funds linked to politically exposed persons, including connections to Azerbaijan. Allegations included weak CDD practices, poor handling of politically sensitive accounts, and inadequate supervisory responses. By opening the investigation, the EBA exercised its powers to review whether a national financial intelligence unit had effectively applied EU AML rules. This case marked one of the earliest examples of EU‚Äëlevel scrutiny over domestic AML supervision, signaling that deficiencies in smaller jurisdictions can draw continent‚Äëwide concern. For multinational compliance teams, it underscored the importance of monitoring supervisory actions in host countries and anticipating reputational fallout.

Source: European Banking Authority notice of investigation, 7 Jun 2018.

Story 6

FATF Launches Work on Virtual Assets/VASPs

Jun 2018: FATF kicks off a project to clarify and strengthen standards for virtual assets and service providers across jurisdictions.

Read the short extract

During the June 2018 Plenary, the FATF announced a new workstream to clarify and strengthen global standards for virtual assets and virtual asset service providers (VASPs). Recognizing the rapid rise of cryptocurrencies, the FATF acknowledged regulatory inconsistencies across jurisdictions and the growing use of virtual assets by criminal groups. The project aimed to define clear criteria for licensing and registration of VASPs, develop enhanced due diligence requirements, and extend suspicious activity reporting frameworks into the crypto sector. Early discussions also raised the possibility of a global ‚ÄúTravel Rule‚Äù for virtual assets, requiring exchanges and wallet providers to share sender and receiver information. For AML compliance officers, this marked the start of a major paradigm shift: crypto service providers would soon face the same regulatory expectations as banks, meaning onboarding, monitoring, and reporting rules had to evolve rapidly.

Source: FATF Plenary Outcomes, Paris, 29 Jun 2018.
