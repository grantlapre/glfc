AML Insight ‚Äî Organ Trafficking

The sale of human organs remains a largely hidden component of human trafficking. High global demand, low supply, and weak oversight in some jurisdictions create the conditions for transnational criminal networks to exploit vulnerable donors and recipients ‚Äî with funds often flowing through legitimate financial channels.

Read the short extract

Organ trafficking operates in the shadows of human trafficking, driven by economics: low supply and high demand elevate prices and attract organised networks. Estimates cited place illicit transplants at a notable share of global procedures, with kidneys prominent. Traffickers recruit vulnerable donors, route procedures through clandestine clinics or reputable hospitals, and sanitise payments through charities, travel and medical tourism intermediaries, and shell companies. These patterns expose financial institutions to facilitation risks unless onboarding, monitoring, and escalation controls surface the warning signs.
